## Overview

Motion tokens define the timing and easing curves used for transitions and animations across the UI. Consistent motion makes the interface feel cohesive and predictable.

## Usage principles

- Use short durations for inline interactions such as hover, focus, and toggle. Use longer durations only when a significant layout change is happening.
- Pair `ease-out` with elements entering the screen and `ease-in` with elements leaving. This matches natural movement and avoids abrupt endings.
- Animate `transform` and `opacity` — not `width`, `height`, or positional properties. Layout-affecting properties force reflows and degrade performance.
- Always support `prefers-reduced-motion`. When this preference is active, remove or significantly reduce all non-essential animations.
