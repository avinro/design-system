# Badge Partner

## Overview

Badge Partner is a small pill-shaped label with a leading icon used on Venue partner cards to communicate a venue's booking capability. It appears in two types: `bookable` (the venue accepts direct bookings) and `request only` (the venue requires a booking request before confirmation).

Badge Partner is a display-only component. It is not interactive and has no pressed, hover, or focus states. It shares the same pill shape and typography as the main Badge component but adds a leading icon slot and uses domain-specific color types.

## Anatomy

Badge Partner is composed of three parts:

| Part | Required | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Container** | Yes | Pill-shaped surface (border-radius: 9999px). Background determined by type. Padding: 8px horizontal, 4px vertical. |
| **Icon** | Yes | Leading icon, 16px. Decorative — communicates type at a glance alongside the label. Must be `aria-hidden`. |
| **Label** | Yes | Short text string. Manrope SemiBold, 12px, `whitespace-nowrap`. Color determined by type. |

- The icon is always required and always precedes the label.
- The label is always required. Do not render Badge Partner without visible text.
- Labels are fixed per type: `"Bookable"` and `"Request only"`. Do not substitute custom text.

## Variants

Badge Partner has two types. Each communicates a distinct booking capability.

| Type | Color | Icon | Label | Semantics |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `bookable` | Mint green | ✓ Checkmark | "Bookable" | The venue is fully bookable — users can complete a booking directly. |
| `request only` | Blush pink | 💬 Chat bubble | "Request only" | The venue requires a booking request — availability is not guaranteed at booking time. |

> Use the type that matches the **actual booking capability** of the venue. Do not swap types based on visual preference.

## Usage guidelines

- Use Badge Partner **only on Venue partner cards** to communicate booking capability. Do not repurpose it for other status or category labeling.
- Display **one Badge Partner per venue card**. Do not combine both types on the same card.
- The badge type must reflect the **actual booking capability** of the venue — do not use `bookable` for venues that require a request.
- Place Badge Partner in the card header area or near the venue name for immediate visual context.
- The labels "Bookable" and "Request only" are fixed. Do not modify them or introduce custom labels.
- Do not use Badge Partner outside of the Venue card context. For other status labels, use the main [Badge](/components/core/indicators-and-states/badge/index) component.

## Accessibility

- The leading icon is decorative. Apply `aria-hidden="true"` to it so screen readers skip it.
- The label text must carry the full meaning independently of the icon and color.
- Do not rely on color alone to distinguish between `bookable` and `request only` — the label is always required.

## Do / Don't

**Do:**
- Use Badge Partner exclusively on Venue partner cards.
- Use the type that matches the venue's actual booking capability.
- Keep the labels fixed: "Bookable" and "Request only".
- Ensure the label is always visible alongside the icon.

**Don't:**
- Don't use Badge Partner outside of Venue card contexts — use the main Badge for general status labeling.
- Don't display both types on the same card simultaneously.
- Don't modify or translate the fixed labels without a confirmed design decision.
- Don't rely on color or icon alone — the label text must always be present.
- Don't add additional types without a confirmed design decision in Figma.
